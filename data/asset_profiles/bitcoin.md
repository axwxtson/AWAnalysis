# Bitcoin (BTC)

Bitcoin is the original cryptocurrency, launched in January 2009 by the
pseudonymous Satoshi Nakamoto. It is the largest crypto asset by market
capitalisation and is widely regarded as a digital store of value.

## What it is

Bitcoin is a peer-to-peer digital cash system that runs on a decentralised
network of computers. Transactions are recorded on a public ledger called
the blockchain, secured through a proof-of-work consensus mechanism in
which miners expend computational energy to validate blocks.

## Why it exists

Bitcoin was designed as a response to the 2008 financial crisis,
proposing money that does not require trust in banks or governments. Its
fixed supply of 21 million coins and predictable issuance schedule are
intended to provide a hedge against monetary inflation.

## Key facts

- **Consensus**: Proof of Work (SHA-256)
- **Supply**: Capped at 21 million BTC
- **Block time**: ~10 minutes
- **Halving**: Every 210,000 blocks (~4 years), reducing block reward
- **Founder**: Satoshi Nakamoto (pseudonymous, identity unknown)
- **Genesis block**: 3 January 2009

## Notable context

Bitcoin's price history has been characterised by sharp cycles tied to
its halving events. It crossed $1,000 in 2013, $20,000 in 2017, and
$100,000 in late 2024. Major institutional milestones include the launch
of CME futures (2017) and the approval of US spot Bitcoin ETFs (January
2024). Bitcoin is the deepest, most liquid crypto market and tends to
lead price action across the broader crypto sector.
