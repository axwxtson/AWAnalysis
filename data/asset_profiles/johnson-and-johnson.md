# Johnson & Johnson (JNJ)

Johnson & Johnson is a multinational healthcare company founded in 1886,
one of the oldest and largest firms in its industry. It trades on the NYSE
under the ticker JNJ.

## What it is

Following the 2023 spin-off of its consumer-health business as Kenvue,
Johnson & Johnson focuses on two segments: Innovative Medicine
(pharmaceuticals across areas such as oncology, immunology, and
neuroscience) and MedTech (medical devices and surgical technologies). It
operates globally across both.

## Why it exists

J&J's strategy is built on diversified, science-led healthcare: spreading
across pharmaceuticals and medical devices to balance the patent cycles and
risks of any single product. Its scale, research pipeline, and long
operating history underpin a reputation for stability within the sector.

## Key facts

- **Sector**: Health Care
- **Exchange**: NYSE
- **Ticker**: JNJ
- **Founded**: 1886, New Brunswick, New Jersey
- **Founders**: Robert Wood Johnson, James Wood Johnson, Edward Mead Johnson
- **NYSE listing**: 1944

## Notable context

Johnson & Johnson is one of a small number of companies with a decades-long
record of consecutive dividend increases, long treated as a defensive
healthcare holding. The 2023 separation of Kenvue refocused the company on
higher-growth pharmaceuticals and medical devices, a significant structural
change to its long-standing three-segment model.
