# Visa Inc. (V)

Visa is a global payments-technology company that operates one of the
world's largest electronic-payments networks. It trades on the NYSE under
the ticker V. Visa does not issue cards or lend to consumers; it runs the
network that moves transactions between banks.

## What it is

Visa operates VisaNet, the processing network that authorises, clears, and
settles card payments between issuing banks, merchants, and acquiring
banks. It earns fees on the volume and number of transactions flowing
through its network rather than from lending, which keeps its model
capital-light and high-margin.

## Why it exists

Visa exists to provide the shared infrastructure that makes electronic
payments work across institutions and borders. Its network benefits from
powerful scale effects: more cardholders attract more merchants and vice
versa, reinforcing its position as a default rail for global commerce.

## Key facts

- **Sector**: Financial services / payments technology
- **Exchange**: NYSE
- **Ticker**: V
- **Origins**: Grew out of Bank of America's BankAmericard (launched 1958);
  became Visa in 1976
- **IPO**: March 2008 — one of the largest US IPOs at the time
- **Headquarters**: San Francisco, California

## Notable context

Visa's 2008 listing came just as the financial crisis was unfolding, yet
its transaction-fee model proved resilient. As a near-pure play on the
secular shift from cash to electronic payments, it is often paired with
Mastercard as a duopoly at the centre of global card networks.
