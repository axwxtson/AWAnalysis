# Chainlink (LINK)

Chainlink is a decentralised oracle network that connects blockchains
to off-chain data, computation, and external systems. It is the
dominant oracle provider in the smart-contract ecosystem.

## What it is

Chainlink is not a standalone blockchain — it is middleware. Networks
of node operators retrieve data from external sources (price feeds,
weather data, sports results, payment confirmations) and deliver them
on-chain in a verifiable way. Smart contracts on Ethereum and other
chains use Chainlink data feeds to make decisions about prices,
liquidations, settlements, and more.

## Why it exists

Smart contracts cannot directly access data outside their own chain.
Chainlink was created to solve this "oracle problem" by providing a
decentralised, tamper-resistant way to bring real-world information
on-chain — the foundation for any DeFi protocol that needs accurate
prices, any insurance protocol that needs event data, and any tokenised
asset that mirrors something off-chain.

## Key facts

- **Type**: Decentralised oracle network (not a blockchain)
- **Native token**: LINK (used to pay node operators and as collateral)
- **Supply**: Capped at 1 billion LINK
- **Co-founder**: Sergey Nazarov (with Steve Ellis)
- **Mainnet launch**: May 2019

## Notable context

Chainlink's price feeds secure the majority of DeFi total value locked.
Its Cross-Chain Interoperability Protocol (CCIP), launched in 2023,
extends its role into messaging between chains. Despite this central
infrastructural role, LINK has often underperformed during crypto bull
runs, with critics pointing to slow token utility growth relative to
network usage.
