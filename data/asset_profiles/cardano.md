# Cardano (ADA)

Cardano is a proof-of-stake blockchain platform developed with a research
-driven, peer-reviewed approach. Its native asset, ADA, is used for
staking, governance, and transaction fees.

## What it is

Cardano is a layer-1 blockchain that supports smart contracts through
its Plutus platform. It is built in Haskell and emphasises formal
verification, with development governed by Input Output Global (IOG),
the Cardano Foundation, and Emurgo.

## Why it exists

Cardano was founded by Charles Hoskinson, a co-founder of Ethereum, who
left in 2014 over disagreements about Ethereum's direction. The project
positions itself as a "third-generation" blockchain that learns from
the design limitations of Bitcoin and Ethereum, prioritising academic
rigour and incremental, evidence-based deployment.

## Key facts

- **Consensus**: Ouroboros (Proof of Stake)
- **Supply**: Capped at 45 billion ADA
- **Block time**: ~20 seconds
- **Founder**: Charles Hoskinson
- **Mainnet launch**: September 2017

## Notable context

Cardano's deliberate development pace has been both praised and
criticised: smart contracts launched only in September 2021, six years
after Ethereum's. Adoption has lagged competitors like Ethereum and
Solana in DeFi total value locked, though the network remains active in
developing-world payments and identity initiatives. ADA reached its
all-time high of around $3.10 in September 2021.
