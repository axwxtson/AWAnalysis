# XRP (XRP)

XRP is the native asset of the XRP Ledger, a decentralised payment
network developed primarily for fast, low-cost cross-border payments.
It is closely associated with Ripple, the company that uses the XRP
Ledger in its enterprise payment products.

## What it is

The XRP Ledger is an open-source, permissionless blockchain that
predates most current crypto networks, having launched in 2012. It uses
a unique consensus protocol — the XRP Ledger Consensus Protocol —
rather than proof of work or proof of stake. Transactions settle in
3–5 seconds at fractions of a cent.

## Why it exists

XRP was created to solve the inefficiency of the international payments
system, which historically relied on correspondent banking, SWIFT
messaging, and pre-funded nostro accounts. The XRP Ledger and Ripple's
products aim to replace this with on-demand liquidity using XRP as a
bridge currency between fiat pairs.

## Key facts

- **Consensus**: XRP Ledger Consensus Protocol (federated agreement)
- **Supply**: Capped at 100 billion XRP, all pre-mined at launch
- **Settlement time**: 3–5 seconds
- **Founders**: Jed McCaleb, David Schwartz, Arthur Britto (Ripple
  co-founded by Chris Larsen and Brad Garlinghouse)
- **Network launch**: 2012

## Notable context

XRP was the subject of a long-running lawsuit between the SEC and Ripple,
which began in December 2020 and largely resolved in mid-2023 with a
mixed ruling — programmatic sales of XRP on exchanges were not deemed
securities, while institutional sales were. The ruling was a
significant moment in US crypto regulation and reinforced the precedent
that secondary market crypto sales may not constitute securities
transactions.
