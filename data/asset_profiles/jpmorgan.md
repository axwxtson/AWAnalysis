# JPMorgan Chase & Co. (JPM)

JPMorgan Chase is the largest bank in the United States by assets, formed
in its modern shape by a 2000 merger but with roots stretching back over
two centuries. It trades on the NYSE under the ticker JPM.

## What it is

JPMorgan Chase is a diversified financial-services firm operating across
consumer and community banking, corporate and investment banking,
commercial banking, and asset and wealth management. It serves retail
customers, corporations, governments, and institutions, and is designated
a globally systemically important bank.

## Why it exists

The firm's scale and diversification are central to its strategy: spreading
across lending, markets, payments, and asset management lets it generate
revenue across economic cycles. Its size and balance-sheet strength make it
a core institution in the US and global financial system.

## Key facts

- **Sector**: Financials (Banking)
- **Exchange**: NYSE
- **Ticker**: JPM
- **Formed**: 2000, via the merger of J.P. Morgan & Co. and Chase Manhattan
- **Heritage**: J.P. Morgan & Co. founded by John Pierpont Morgan;
  predecessor banks date to 1799
- **Headquarters**: New York City

## Notable context

JPMorgan emerged from the 2008 financial crisis comparatively strong,
acquiring distressed institutions, and is widely watched as a barometer of
the US economy through its lending and consumer-spending data. Its annual
shareholder letters are closely followed for commentary on markets and the
economy.
