# Apple Inc. (AAPL)

Apple is a multinational technology company founded in 1976, best known
for the iPhone, Mac, and a tightly integrated ecosystem of hardware,
software, and services. It is one of the largest publicly traded
companies in the world by market capitalisation and trades on the NASDAQ
under the ticker AAPL.

## What it is

Apple designs, manufactures, and markets consumer electronics, software,
and online services. Its core products include the iPhone, iPad, Mac,
Apple Watch, and AirPods, alongside a services segment spanning the App
Store, iCloud, Apple Music, and Apple Pay. The company is vertically
integrated, designing its own silicon (the M-series and A-series chips)
and operating systems.

## Why it exists

Apple's enduring strategy is to control the full stack — hardware,
software, and services — to deliver a seamless user experience and
capture recurring revenue across a large installed base. The services
business, built on more than a billion active devices, has become a
central driver of margin and growth as hardware sales have matured.

## Key facts

- **Sector**: Technology (Consumer Electronics)
- **Exchange**: NASDAQ
- **Ticker**: AAPL
- **Founded**: 1976, Cupertino, California
- **Founders**: Steve Jobs, Steve Wozniak, Ronald Wayne
- **IPO**: December 1980

## Notable context

Apple was the first US company to reach a $1 trillion market
capitalisation (2018), later crossing $2tn and $3tn milestones. Revenue
is historically concentrated in the iPhone, with services increasingly
central to the growth story. It is one of the most heavily weighted
constituents of the S&P 500 and NASDAQ-100, so its moves carry
index-level influence.
