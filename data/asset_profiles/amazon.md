# Amazon.com, Inc. (AMZN)

Amazon is a multinational technology and e-commerce company founded in
1994. It began as an online bookseller and grew into one of the world's
largest retailers and cloud providers. It trades on the NASDAQ under the
ticker AMZN.

## What it is

Amazon operates a vast online marketplace, a logistics and fulfilment
network, a subscription programme (Prime), a fast-growing advertising
business, and Amazon Web Services (AWS), the cloud-infrastructure division
that is a major profit driver. It also produces devices and content
through Kindle, Echo, and Prime Video.

## Why it exists

Amazon's founding philosophy prioritises long-term customer obsession and
reinvestment over short-term profit. That approach funded its expansion
from retail into cloud, advertising, and logistics. AWS, in particular,
turned spare internal infrastructure capability into one of the most
profitable cloud businesses in the industry.

## Key facts

- **Sector**: Consumer Discretionary (with AWS in cloud computing)
- **Exchange**: NASDAQ
- **Ticker**: AMZN
- **Founded**: 1994, Seattle, Washington
- **Founder**: Jeff Bezos
- **IPO**: May 1997

## Notable context

Amazon's relentless reinvestment kept reported profits modest for years
even as revenue scaled enormously, with AWS later becoming the engine
behind its margins. It is among the largest US companies by market value
and a bellwether for both consumer spending and cloud-infrastructure
demand.
