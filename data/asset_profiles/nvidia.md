# NVIDIA Corporation (NVDA)

NVIDIA is a semiconductor company founded in 1993, best known for the
graphics processing units (GPUs) that power gaming, professional
visualisation, and — increasingly — artificial intelligence. It trades on
the NASDAQ under the ticker NVDA.

## What it is

NVIDIA designs GPUs and the surrounding software ecosystem. Its chips
accelerate workloads from real-time graphics to large-scale machine
learning, and its CUDA software platform has become a de facto standard
for general-purpose GPU computing. The company sells into gaming, data
centres, professional visualisation, and automotive markets.

## Why it exists

NVIDIA's long bet was that parallel computation on GPUs would matter far
beyond graphics. That bet paid off as machine learning came to rely on
the massive parallelism GPUs provide, making NVIDIA a central supplier of
the hardware behind modern AI. Its combination of chips and the CUDA
software moat is core to its position.

## Key facts

- **Sector**: Technology (Semiconductors)
- **Exchange**: NASDAQ
- **Ticker**: NVDA
- **Founded**: 1993, Santa Clara, California
- **Founders**: Jensen Huang, Chris Malachowsky, Curtis Priem
- **IPO**: January 1999

## Notable context

NVIDIA became one of the most valuable companies in the world on the back
of demand for AI accelerators, joining the small group of US firms to
cross multi-trillion-dollar valuations. Its data-centre business now
rivals or exceeds its historical gaming roots, and its results are widely
watched as a barometer for AI infrastructure spending.
