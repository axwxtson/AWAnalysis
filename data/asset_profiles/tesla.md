# Tesla, Inc. (TSLA)

Tesla is an electric-vehicle and clean-energy company founded in 2003. It
designs and manufactures electric cars, battery energy-storage systems,
and solar products. It trades on the NASDAQ under the ticker TSLA.

## What it is

Tesla's core business is designing, manufacturing, and selling electric
vehicles, supported by its own charging network and software. Alongside
automotive, it sells energy-generation and storage products such as
Powerwall and large-scale battery installations, and it develops
driver-assistance software.

## Why it exists

Tesla's stated mission is to accelerate the world's transition to
sustainable energy. By building desirable electric vehicles at scale, it
aimed to push the wider auto industry towards electrification, using
vertical integration in batteries, software, and manufacturing as
competitive advantages.

## Key facts

- **Sector**: Consumer Discretionary (Automobiles)
- **Exchange**: NASDAQ
- **Ticker**: TSLA
- **Founded**: 2003, San Carlos, California (now Austin, Texas)
- **Founders**: Martin Eberhard and Marc Tarpenning (Elon Musk joined
  early as chairman and lead investor, later becoming CEO)
- **IPO**: June 2010

## Notable context

Tesla was the first US carmaker to go public in decades when it listed in
2010, and its valuation later exceeded that of established automakers
combined despite far lower production volumes. Its share price is unusually
volatile and closely tied to sentiment about growth, autonomy, and its
high-profile CEO.
