# Alphabet Inc. (GOOGL)

Alphabet is the holding company for Google, created in a 2015
restructuring. Google itself was founded in 1998. Alphabet trades on the
NASDAQ, with Class A shares under the ticker GOOGL and Class C shares
under GOOG.

## What it is

Alphabet's business is dominated by Google: search, the advertising
network built around it, YouTube, the Android mobile operating system,
the Chrome browser, and Google Cloud. Beyond Google, Alphabet houses
"Other Bets" such as the autonomous-driving unit Waymo. Advertising
remains the company's primary revenue engine.

## Why it exists

The 2015 Alphabet structure was designed to separate Google's core,
profitable advertising and platform businesses from longer-horizon,
experimental ventures, giving each clearer accountability. Google's
strategy rests on organising information and monetising attention at
enormous scale, with cloud and AI as growth priorities.

## Key facts

- **Sector**: Communication Services
- **Exchange**: NASDAQ
- **Tickers**: GOOGL (Class A), GOOG (Class C)
- **Founded**: Google 1998; Alphabet holding structure 2015
- **Founders**: Larry Page, Sergey Brin
- **IPO**: Google listed in August 2004

## Notable context

Google's 2004 IPO used an unusual auction process and valued the company
at a level that has since multiplied many times over. Alphabet is one of
the largest companies in the world and a dominant force in digital
advertising, with regulatory scrutiny of that dominance a recurring theme.
