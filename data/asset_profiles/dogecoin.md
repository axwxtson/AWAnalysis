# Dogecoin (DOGE)

Dogecoin is a proof-of-work cryptocurrency originally created as a joke
in 2013, based on the Shiba Inu "Doge" internet meme. Despite its
satirical origin, it has persisted as one of the largest cryptocurrencies
by market capitalisation.

## What it is

Dogecoin is a fork of Litecoin (which itself is a fork of Bitcoin), with
modifications that produce faster block times and a much larger supply.
It uses Scrypt-based proof of work, the same algorithm as Litecoin, and
is often "merge-mined" alongside it.

## Why it exists

Dogecoin was created by Billy Markus and Jackson Palmer as a parody of
the speculative excess in the cryptocurrency space. It was deliberately
designed to be inflationary and friendly to small transactions, with
the community emphasising tipping, charity drives, and meme culture
rather than serious technical innovation.

## Key facts

- **Consensus**: Proof of Work (Scrypt)
- **Supply**: Uncapped, with ~5 billion new DOGE issued per year
- **Block time**: ~1 minute
- **Founders**: Billy Markus and Jackson Palmer
- **Launch**: December 2013

## Notable context

Dogecoin gained mainstream attention during the 2021 retail trading
boom, driven heavily by Elon Musk's tweets and references on Saturday
Night Live. It briefly reached around $0.74, giving it a market
capitalisation rivalling major equities. Despite minimal active
development, it remains a top-ten asset by market cap and a touchstone
of crypto meme culture.
