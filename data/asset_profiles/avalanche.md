# Avalanche (AVAX)

Avalanche is a high-performance smart-contract platform built around a
novel consensus mechanism and a multi-chain architecture. It is designed
to combine Ethereum compatibility with significantly faster finality.

## What it is

Avalanche is a layer-1 blockchain platform composed of three integrated
chains: the X-Chain (asset transfers), the C-Chain (Ethereum-compatible
smart contracts), and the P-Chain (validators and subnets). Its
consensus mechanism, Avalanche Consensus, achieves sub-second finality
through repeated random sampling among validators.

## Why it exists

Avalanche was created by Emin Gün Sirer, a Cornell professor with deep
research in distributed systems. It targets institutional and
enterprise use cases where deterministic finality and customisable
"subnets" matter — for example, regulated DeFi, on-chain capital
markets, and gaming networks that need their own rule sets.

## Key facts

- **Consensus**: Avalanche Consensus (Proof of Stake variant)
- **Supply**: Capped at 720 million AVAX
- **Finality**: Sub-second
- **Founder**: Emin Gün Sirer (Ava Labs)
- **Mainnet launch**: September 2020

## Notable context

Avalanche grew rapidly in 2021–2022 as DeFi spilled over from Ethereum
seeking lower fees. Its subnet model has been picked up for institutional
pilots including JPMorgan's Onyx-related experiments. AVAX peaked above
$140 in late 2021 before falling sharply in the 2022 bear market. The
ecosystem remains a meaningful Ethereum alternative for EVM-compatible
DeFi.
