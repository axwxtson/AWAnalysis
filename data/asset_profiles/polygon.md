# Polygon (MATIC)

Polygon is a layer-2 scaling ecosystem for Ethereum, originally launched
as the Matic Network. Its native token MATIC is used for staking,
governance, and gas fees on the Polygon PoS chain.

## What it is

Polygon is a suite of scaling solutions for Ethereum. The flagship is
Polygon PoS, a sidechain compatible with the Ethereum Virtual Machine
that processes transactions cheaply and quickly while periodically
checkpointing to Ethereum. The broader Polygon stack now includes zkEVM,
Polygon CDK, and the AggLayer for cross-chain coordination.

## Why it exists

In Ethereum's high-fee periods, ordinary transactions could cost tens
of dollars. Polygon was designed to give Ethereum users a near-identical
developer experience at a tiny fraction of the cost, capturing
applications that needed Ethereum's security profile without its
mainnet fees.

## Key facts

- **Type**: Ethereum scaling ecosystem (sidechain + ZK rollup + SDK)
- **Native token**: MATIC (transitioning to POL under Polygon 2.0)
- **Supply**: Capped at 10 billion MATIC
- **Founders**: Jaynti Kanani, Sandeep Nailwal, Anurag Arjun (and others)
- **Mainnet launch**: May 2020 (as Matic Network); rebranded to Polygon
  in February 2021

## Notable context

Polygon was an early winner of the layer-2 narrative, hosting major
applications like Aave, Uniswap, and OpenSea Polygon markets. The
project announced Polygon 2.0 in 2023, including a token migration from
MATIC to POL and a more unified architecture. MATIC reached its peak
near $2.90 in December 2021 before retreating with the broader market.
