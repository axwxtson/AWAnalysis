# Meta Platforms, Inc. (META)

Meta is a multinational technology company founded in 2004 as Facebook and
renamed Meta Platforms in 2021. It operates the world's largest family of
social-media and messaging services. It trades on the NASDAQ under the
ticker META.

## What it is

Meta runs Facebook, Instagram, WhatsApp, and Messenger, reaching billions
of users, and earns the large majority of its revenue from advertising
sold against that audience. Its Reality Labs division develops virtual-
and augmented-reality hardware and the longer-term "metaverse" vision that
prompted the 2021 rename.

## Why it exists

Meta's core business monetises social connection and attention at global
scale through targeted advertising. The 2021 rebrand signalled a strategic
bet that immersive computing — VR/AR and the metaverse — could be the next
major platform, funded by the cash flows of the advertising business.

## Key facts

- **Sector**: Communication Services
- **Exchange**: NASDAQ
- **Ticker**: META
- **Founded**: 2004, Cambridge, Massachusetts (now Menlo Park, California)
- **Founders**: Mark Zuckerberg and co-founders incl. Eduardo Saverin,
  Dustin Moskovitz, Chris Hughes, Andrew McCollum
- **IPO**: May 2012 (as Facebook)

## Notable context

Facebook's 2012 IPO was among the largest in technology history. The
company has navigated repeated debates over privacy, content moderation,
and competition, while its heavy Reality Labs investment remains a
contested element of its strategy. It is one of the most valuable
advertising businesses in the world.
