# Polkadot (DOT)

Polkadot is a multi-chain protocol designed to allow specialised
blockchains ("parachains") to interoperate within a shared security
model. It was founded by Gavin Wood, who previously co-founded Ethereum
and authored its yellow paper.

## What it is

Polkadot is composed of a central "relay chain" that provides shared
security and consensus, with parachains connected to it that can be
customised for specific use cases. Validators secure the relay chain;
collators produce blocks for parachains. The native DOT token is used
for staking, governance, and bonding parachain slots.

## Why it exists

Polkadot was designed to address blockchain interoperability and
specialisation. Rather than one chain trying to do everything, Polkadot
allows different chains to be optimised for different purposes — privacy
chains, identity chains, DeFi chains — while inheriting security from
the relay chain.

## Key facts

- **Consensus**: Nominated Proof of Stake (NPoS) with GRANDPA finality
- **Supply**: Uncapped, with ~10% target inflation adjusted by staking ratio
- **Block time**: ~6 seconds (relay chain)
- **Founder**: Gavin Wood (Web3 Foundation, Parity Technologies)
- **Mainnet launch**: May 2020

## Notable context

Polkadot's parachain auction model launched in late 2021, with projects
bonding DOT to win parachain slots. Adoption of parachains has been
slower than initial expectations, and DOT has underperformed the broader
crypto market since its 2021 highs around $55. The ecosystem remains
technically respected but struggles for retail mindshare against more
performant alternatives.
