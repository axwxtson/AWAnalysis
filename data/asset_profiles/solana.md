# Solana (SOL)

Solana is a high-throughput layer-1 blockchain known for low transaction
fees and fast finality. It positions itself as a performance-focused
alternative to Ethereum for consumer applications, DeFi, and on-chain
trading.

## What it is

Solana is a proof-of-stake blockchain designed for high transaction
throughput. It uses a novel mechanism called Proof of History — a
cryptographic clock that orders transactions before consensus — to
enable parallel processing of transactions and theoretical throughput
in the tens of thousands of transactions per second.

## Why it exists

Solana was created to solve crypto's scalability problem without relying
on layer-2 rollups or sharding. By engineering a single fast layer-1, it
aims to host applications that need low latency and high frequency, such
as on-chain order books, gaming, and consumer payments.

## Key facts

- **Consensus**: Proof of Stake with Proof of History (PoH)
- **Supply**: ~580 million SOL (with ongoing inflation)
- **Block time**: ~400 milliseconds
- **Founder**: Anatoly Yakovenko
- **Mainnet launch**: March 2020

## Notable context

Solana has experienced multiple network outages, particularly during
2021–2022 under heavy load, raising questions about its reliability
trade-offs. The network's reputation suffered during the FTX collapse
(November 2022) given Sam Bankman-Fried's prominent backing. SOL fell
over 95% from its 2021 peak before recovering strongly into 2024,
driven by memecoin trading and renewed developer activity. Solana has
the largest active user base by daily transactions in crypto.
