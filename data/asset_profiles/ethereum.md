# Ethereum (ETH)

Ethereum is a decentralised computing platform that introduced
programmable smart contracts to the cryptocurrency space. It is the
second-largest crypto asset by market capitalisation and the foundation
of most decentralised finance (DeFi) and NFT activity.

## What it is

Ethereum is a blockchain that supports arbitrary computation through
smart contracts — self-executing code deployed on-chain. Its native
asset, ether (ETH), is used to pay for transaction execution (gas fees)
and is also held as an investment asset and store of value.

## Why it exists

Ethereum was proposed by Vitalik Buterin in 2013 to extend Bitcoin's
ledger model to support general-purpose programs. This enabled an
ecosystem of applications — lending markets, decentralised exchanges,
stablecoins, and tokenised assets — that cannot exist on Bitcoin.

## Key facts

- **Consensus**: Proof of Stake (since the Merge, September 2022)
- **Supply**: No hard cap; issuance offset by burning (EIP-1559)
- **Block time**: ~12 seconds
- **Native asset**: Ether (ETH)
- **Founder**: Vitalik Buterin (with co-founders Gavin Wood, Joseph Lubin
  and others)
- **Mainnet launch**: 30 July 2015

## Notable context

Ethereum has gone through several major upgrades: the DAO fork (2016)
which split the network into ETH and ETH Classic; EIP-1559 (2021) which
introduced fee burning; and the Merge (2022) which switched consensus
from proof of work to proof of stake, cutting energy use by ~99.95%.
Ethereum is the dominant smart-contract platform by total value locked
in DeFi and is a core layer of crypto infrastructure.
